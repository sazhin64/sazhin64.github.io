When I was a child, my friends called me “story teller” not because I told lies, but because I was good at telling stories. Actually, most of the plots of these stories were based on facts from my own life. There are so many young people living so far from nature that they seldom see the things I speak about. They appreciated all my imaginative, yet real, stories.
I gave the places I loved special names. My happiest days were linked to secret hideouts nobody knew of their existence, but a few close friends and relatives. Every morning on my way out I said, “Today I am going to Muskrat Hats.” In my mind, I imagined living trees with folded out barks, dwelling to snakes and crocodiles. A huge pike in clear waters looked to me, as a little boy, like a bloodthirsty beast. I wanted to catch it, but I was afraid it was going to chew away my hook. I became so obsessed with this pike that I
watched its moves every free time I got. Happy and proud I finally passed by my neighbor’s window: the pike still had the hook in its mouth. I named this place “The Nook Of The Frightened Pikes.” And thus, the list of geographical names grew fast. Everything my eyes capture is firmly imprinted in my memory. Most important of all was the feeling of freedom that I enjoyed as a child. Then childhood a teen years are gone and now I am inducted, my hands feeling the cold metal of a machine gun instead. My legs get cold along the snow-covered road. My eyes, though, stay on the sides of the river dotted with flowers. I know every herbal shoot. No. Nobody can take those real dreams away from me. I had a boy, and as soon as he could, I showed him my secret places. It was in my river, where he learned how to swim and fish, and there he also caught his first pike. I intend to preserve my tradition and the habit individual naming of favorite places will be followed by my children and grandchildren, hoping they make up new names for their secret places.

Everybody have his own little world on map to touch his heart. For me it is a tiny Brigadoon* missing within the Ural forests.
*The legendary tale of Brigadoon, a small town in a forest in the Scottish</em>
highlands, that appears for one day every century, unchanged by history and</em>
untouched by progress.</em>

My lovest place.
