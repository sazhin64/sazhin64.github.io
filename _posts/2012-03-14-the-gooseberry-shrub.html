A warm, humid, blooming spring promised a good harvest. A hot, dry summer followed —
no rain.  I was impatiently waiting to go on vacation to the village where I was born and spent my childhood. My goal contradicted my intentions, I was not that happy.  As I was lying on the sofa before going to bed, I painted in my mind images from  the past, the people who are still alive and those who had already passed on. I get a  ticket. I am, as usual, full of emotions. I am sitting by the window, looking for any known place, river, old trees and elbows on the road. What is ahead for me? I want to see again those familiar places, thinking they have not changed. Yet, one cannot swim in the same water twice. I recall those happy days when I was a child and a teen-ager. Despite the changes, the old house still stands up in our yard; the old trees still in the garden, and the road through I walked with no shoes on was still winding around the place. Some berries were ripening in the gardens, I get some in my mouth and feel its tart taste. I see with pleasure the ripened strawberry and currant  shrubs. The old gooseberry plant  survived the fire destroied our old house. I ponder about Nature’s wonderful gifts to man, waiting to be accepted. The gooseberry branches brought the boy in me back to school days.  I plan to go home early in the morning. It is  still dark and I am already in the bus yet I imagine grandma popping in with a pack of red gooseberries. I could perceive her tears, even at a distance, when her figure stood on the road until I could not see her any more. I am man now. Still I see how grandma’s fingers gathering berries for her grandson felt no pain from the pricking of the bush thorns. And although seven years have gone since her death, her facial sweet features are burned in my mind.

my grandma.
18.08.05. Yekaterinburg.
